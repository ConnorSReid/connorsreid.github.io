What I Believe In

I believe as an engineer, it is my responsibility to ethically engage with society through all facets of the engineering and scientific process. This statement, although personal in regards to my moral opinions, also acts as my "call to action." My goals as an engineer do not stand in singular to simply create, research, or discover; they corroborate with my, albeit, plainly-put moral opinions. As an engineer, I believe I can use my skills to positively impact society and that if possible to do so—whatever that may be at some time in my life, career, etc.—then I have good reason to attempt at doing so.

Speaking in such a manner seems almost like these beliefs exist on an extreme. But everything in life requires nuance and as such, nothing I personally stand with is firmly set in stone. They exist more so a framework at which I structure my intent in being a engineer.
